Motorola Edge 30 Ultra Review: A Flagship Phone with a 200MP Camera

by John Alvarado - Feb 18 5239 Views 0 Comment

Motorola is not a brand that usually comes to mind when you think of flagship phones. However, the company has recently launched a new device that aims to challenge the likes of Samsung, Apple, and Xiaomi. The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra is a premium phone with some impressive features, such as a 200MP main camera, a 144Hz OLED display, a Snapdragon 8+ Gen 1 processor, and a 125W fast charging support. But how does it perform in real life? Is it worth buying? And how does it compare to other phones with 200MP cameras? In this review, I will try to answer these questions and give you my honest opinion about the Motorola Edge 30 Ultra.

Design The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra has a sleek and elegant design that looks and feels premium. The phone has a curved-edge screen that wraps around the sides of the device, giving it an immersive and futuristic look. The back of the phone is made of glass with a textured pattern that adds some grip and style. The phone also has an aluminum frame that adds some durability and rigidity. The phone comes in two colors: black and white. I prefer the black one because it looks more classy and stealthy. The white one is also nice but it may show fingerprints more easily. The phone has a large 6.67-inch display that takes up most of the front panel. There is a small punch-hole cutout for the selfie camera on the top center of the screen. There are also thin bezels around the screen that make it look even more expansive. The back of the phone houses the quad-camera system that consists of four lenses arranged in a rectangular module on the top left corner. There is also an LED flash next to them. The camera bump is not too protruding but it may wobble slightly when placed on a flat surface. The right side of the phone has the power button and volume rocker that are easy to reach and press. The left side has nothing except for an antenna band. The bottom edge has a USB-C port for charging and data transfer, as well as a speaker grille and microphone hole. The top edge has another microphone hole and an IR blaster that can be used to control compatible devices like TVs or air conditioners. The phone does not have a headphone jack or microSD card slot which may disappoint some users who prefer wired headphones or extra storage space. However, these are common omissions in flagship phones nowadays so they are not deal-breakers for me. The phone also has an under-display fingerprint sensor that works well most of the time but sometimes fails to recognize my finger or takes longer than usual to unlock. I wish Motorola had opted for a more reliable side-mounted or rear-mounted sensor instead. The phone also supports wireless charging and reverse wireless charging which are nice features to have especially if you own other devices that can benefit from them. One thing I dont like about the design of the phone is its IP52 rating which means it can only withstand light splashes of water but not submersion or heavy rain. For a flagship phone that costs almost 900, I would expect at least IP67 or IP68 rating which would offer better protection against water damage. This is something Motorola should improve on in future models.

Display The display of the Motorola Edge 30 Ultra is one of its highlights. Its an OLED panel with Full HD+ resolution (1080 x 2400 pixels) which translates into sharp images, vivid colors, and deep blacks. It also supports HDR10+ which enhances contrast and brightness for compatible content. But what makes this display stand out from other flagships is its refresh rate. It can go up to 144Hz which means it can refresh up to 144 times per second, making everything look smoother, faster, and more responsive. This is especially noticeable when scrolling through web pages, playing games, or watching videos. You can also adjust the refresh rate manually between 60Hz, 90Hz, 120Hz, or auto mode which adapts according to what youre doing on your screen. Another feature I like about this display is its curved edges which create an immersive viewing experience by extending beyond the flat surface of the screen. They also add some functionality by allowing you to access shortcuts, notifications, or settings by swiping from either edge. You can customize these edge features according to your preferences in settings. However, there are some drawbacks to having curved edges too such as accidental touches, glare, or distortion. Sometimes I would unintentionally launch an app or change a setting by touching the edge of the screen. Other times I would see some reflections or color shifts on the curved parts of the screen. These are minor issues that can be avoided by using a case or adjusting your grip, but they are still worth mentioning. Overall, I think the display of the Motorola Edge 30 Ultra is one of the best Ive seen on a smartphone. Its bright, colorful, smooth, and immersive. Its also well calibrated and has several modes to adjust the color temperature and saturation according to your liking.

The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra has a quad-camera system on the back that consists of a 200MP main camera, a 50MP ultra-wide camera, a 12MP telephoto camera with 2x optical zoom, and an 8MP depth sensor for portrait mode. On the front, it has a 60MP selfie camera. The main camera uses pixel binning to combine nine pixels into one and produce 22MP images by default. This helps reduce noise and improve dynamic range in low-light situations. You can also switch to full resolution mode if you want to capture more details and crop later. The main camera takes great photos in most scenarios. The colors are accurate and natural, the exposure is balanced, and the details are sharp. The dynamic range is also impressive as it can handle both bright highlights and dark shadows well. The autofocus is fast and reliable too. However, there are some drawbacks to having such a high-resolution sensor too. For one thing, it takes up more storage space than lower-resolution sensors. For another thing, it doesnt perform well in very low-light situations as it struggles with noise and blur. You can use night mode to improve this but it still falls short compared to other flagships with larger pixels or better software processing. The ultra-wide camera is also very good on this phone. It has a wide field of view (114 degrees) that lets you capture more scenery or people in your shots. It also has autofocus which is rare for ultra-wide cameras and allows you to take macro shots or focus on close objects. The ultra-wide camera produces images that are consistent with the main camera in terms of colors, exposure, and dynamic range. It also retains a lot of details and minimizes distortion on the edges. However, it does suffer from some noise and softness in low-light conditions, so you may want to use night mode or switch to the main camera for better results. The telephoto camera offers 2x optical zoom which is useful for getting closer to distant subjects without losing quality. It also supports digital zoom up to 10x which can be handy for some situations but not very reliable as it introduces noise and artifacts. The telephoto camera performs well in good lighting conditions, delivering clear images with good colors and contrast. However, it struggles in low-light conditions, producing noisy images with poor details and colors. It also lacks optical image stabilization (OIS) which makes it prone to blur from hand movements. The depth sensor helps create portrait mode photos with blurred backgrounds that highlight your subject. You can adjust the level of blur before or after taking the photo using a slider on your screen. You can also apply various effects like bokeh shapes, colors, or lighting styles to enhance your portraits. The portrait mode works well most of the time, creating realistic bokeh effects with accurate edge detection. However, it sometimes fails to separate complex subjects like hair or glasses from the background, resulting in unnatural cutouts or artifacts. The selfie camera has a whopping 60MP resolution which makes it one of the highest-resolution selfie cameras on any smartphone. It also uses pixel binning to produce 15MP images by default which are still very detailed and sharp. You can switch to full resolution mode if you want even more details but be prepared for huge file sizes. The selfie camera takes excellent selfies in most situations. It captures natural skin tones, accurate colors, and balanced exposure. It also has good dynamic range as it can handle backlighting well without blowing out highlights or losing shadows. You can use portrait mode on this camera too which works similarly as on the rear cameras but with slightly less accuracy and consistency. One thing I dont like about this selfie camera is its fixed focus which means you have to hold your phone at an optimal distance from your face (about an arms length) otherwise your selfies will be out of focus or blurry. This can be annoying if you want to take group selfies or selfies at different angles or distances. Overall, I think the cameras of the Motorola Edge 30 Ultra are very impressive and versatile. They can capture great photos in most scenarios and offer a lot of options and features to play with. However, they are not perfect and have some limitations and drawbacks that prevent them from being the best in class. The 200MP main camera is a bit overkill and doesnt offer much advantage over lower-resolution sensors. The telephoto camera could have more zoom range and stabilization. The selfie camera could have autofocus and a wider angle.

Performance and specs The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra is powered by the Snapdragon 8+ Gen 1 processor which is one of the most powerful chipsets available for Android phones. Its an octa-core processor with one Cortex-X2 core clocked at 3.19GHz, three Cortex-A710 cores clocked at 2.75GHz, and four Cortex-A510 cores clocked at 1.8GHz. It also has an Adreno 730 GPU for graphics performance. The phone comes with either 8GB or 12GB of RAM and either 128GB, 256GB, or 512GB of internal storage (UFS 3.1). There is no microSD card slot for expandable storage which may be an issue for some users who need more space for their files or media. The phone runs on Android 12 out of the box which is the latest version of Googles operating system. It has a clean and simple interface that doesnt have much bloatware or unnecessary apps. It also has some Motorola-specific features like Moto Actions, Moto Display, Moto Gametime, Moto Audio, and Ready For. Moto Actions are gestures that let you perform certain actions by moving your phone in certain ways like twisting it to launch the camera or chopping it to turn on the flashlight. Moto Display is a feature that shows you notifications, time, and battery level on your screen when its off or when you pick up your phone. You can also interact with the notifications without unlocking your phone or use peak display to see more information by tapping on the screen. Moto Gametime is a feature that optimizes your gaming experience by blocking interruptions, enhancing performance, and giving you access to various tools like screenshot, screen recorder, or gamepad mode. Moto Audio is a feature that adapts the sound quality according to what youre listening to or where youre listening from. It also lets you customize the equalizer settings according to your preferences. Ready For is a feature that lets you connect your phone to an external monitor or TV using a USB-C cable or wireless connection. It then transforms your phone into a desktop-like experience where you can use apps, browse the web, watch videos, play games, or make video calls on a bigger screen. You can also use your phone as a touchpad or keyboard or connect other accessories like mouse, keyboard, or gamepad. The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra delivers excellent performance in all aspects. It handles multitasking, gaming, and productivity tasks with ease and without any lag or stuttering. It also stays cool and quiet even under heavy load. It scores very high on benchmark tests too, surpassing most other flagships on the market. Software The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra runs on Android 12 out of the box which is the latest version of Googles operating system. It has a clean and simple interface that doesnt have much bloatware or unnecessary apps. It also has some Motorola-specific features like Moto Actions, Moto Display, Moto Gametime, Moto Audio, and Ready For. Moto Actions are gestures that let you perform certain actions by moving your phone in certain ways like twisting it to launch the camera or chopping it to turn on the flashlight. You can also use your phone as a touchpad or keyboard or connect other accessories like mouse, keyboard, or gamepad. The software on this phone is smooth and responsive. It offers a lot of customization options and features that enhance the user experience. It also gets regular updates and security patches from Motorola. However, it does have some drawbacks too. For one thing, it lacks some features that other flagships have like wireless DeX mode (Samsung), Face ID (Apple), or MIUI 13 (Xiaomi). For another thing, it may not get as many major Android updates as other brands promise. Motorola only guarantees one major update for this phone which means it may not get Android 13 or beyond.

Battery life The Motorola Edge 30 Ultra has a 5,000mAh battery which is fairly large for a flagship phone. It also supports fast charging up to 125W which is one of the fastest charging speeds available for smartphones. The phone comes with a 125W charger in the box which can charge the phone from zero to 100% in about 30 minutes. Thats impressive and convenient if you need to top up your battery quickly. The battery life on this phone is excellent too. It can easily last for a full day or more with moderate usage like browsing, messaging, streaming, gaming, or photography. It can also handle heavy usage like gaming for hours without draining too much battery. The high refresh rate display does consume more power than lower refresh rate displays but not significantly so. You can also lower the refresh rate manually or automatically if you want to save some battery life. The phone also supports wireless charging up to 50W which is faster than most other phones that support wireless charging. It also supports reverse wireless charging up to 10W which lets you charge other devices like earbuds or smartwatches by placing them on the back of the phone. Overall, I think the battery life and charging speed of the Motorola Edge 30 Ultra are outstanding and among the best in its class. They offer great convenience and peace of mind for users who dont want to worry about running out of juice or waiting too long for their phones to charge up again.
